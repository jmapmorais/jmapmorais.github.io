FULL BIO

João moved to porto in 2011 where he started his studies on architecture at FAUP. during this period, from 2013 to 2016, he developed design work for the student association, and in 2015 he was one of the founders of Quinzena initiative aiming to question-reform the politics of the educational system.

in 2016, together with Francisco Craveiro and Miguel Royo, founded oficina fumo, an experimental multi-disciplinary practice with a focus on socio-political critique. he collaborated with the other founding members on a performance (2017) and an installation (2016), as well as with João Costa and Manuel Carvalho on two architectural projects (2017 and 2018).

in 2016 joão began his professional practice as an architectural designer with a collaboration with Telmo Castro Arq., as well as, later that year, a direct commission for a residential conversion project in Billancourt–Rives de Seine, Paris. he worked as a furniture designer in 2018-2019. from 2019 to 2020 he collaborated on the production of several private and corporate events performing functions such as set design, ux design and production management. in 2020 joão started collaborating with Vaga Arquitectura as an architect and in 2021 with ATELIERDACOSTA, keeping an external collaborator role on both practices currently.

10 years after leaving his introduction to piano and music theory classes, João begins his musical practice in 2012. since then he worked on different projects ranging from a sound fiction in collaboration with Miguel Royo, to a commissioned piece focused on sleep and sleep time (later remixed in the form of the Concrete-Experimental album VALHALLA), as well as soundtracks for Nicole Maia and Marta Miranda.
